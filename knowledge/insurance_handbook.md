## 2. Motor Insurance

### 2.1 Comprehensive Car Insurance

Covers damage to your own vehicle as well as third-party liability. This is the most complete form of car insurance.

**Own Damage Cover includes:**
- Accidental damage due to collision, overturning, or falling objects
- Fire, explosion, or lightning damage
- Theft of the vehicle
- Natural disasters: floods, earthquakes, storms, cyclones, hurricanes
- Riots, strikes, and malicious acts
- Damage during transit by road, rail, inland waterway, or air
- Personal accident cover for owner-driver: ₹15 Lakh

**Third-Party Liability Cover:**
- Mandatory as per Motor Vehicles Act, 1988
- Covers bodily injury or death of third parties
- Covers damage to third-party property (up to ₹7.5 Lakh)
- Unlimited liability for death or bodily injury

**No Claim Bonus (NCB):**
| Claim-Free Years | NCB Discount |
|-----------------|-------------|
| After 1 year | 20% |
| After 2 years | 25% |
| After 3 years | 35% |
| After 4 years | 45% |
| After 5+ years | 50% |

**NCB Protection Add-on:** For an additional premium of ₹500-₹1,500, protect your NCB even if you make one claim during the year.

**Key Exclusions:**
- Normal wear and tear, mechanical or electrical breakdown
- Damage due to driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Driving without a valid driving license
- Using the vehicle for purposes other than those stated in the policy
- Consequential loss (e.g., loss of income due to vehicle being in the workshop)
- Depreciation on parts replaced during repair

**Depreciation Schedule for Claim Settlement:**
| Vehicle Age | Depreciation on Parts |
|------------|---------------------|
| Less than 6 months | 0% (Nil) |
| 6 months - 1 year | 5% |
| 1-2 years | 10% |
| 2-3 years | 15% |
| 3-4 years | 25% |
| 4-5 years | 35% |
| 5+ years | 50% |

**Zero Depreciation Add-on:** Eliminates depreciation deduction during claims. Available for vehicles up to 5 years old. Costs approximately 15-20% additional premium.

## 6. Claims Process

### 6.1 How to File a Claim

**Step 1: Intimate the Claim**
- Call our 24/7 claims helpline: 1800-123-4567 (toll-free)
- Use the SecureLife mobile app: Claims → File New Claim
- Visit the customer portal: claims.securelife.in
- Visit your nearest branch with policy documents

**Step 2: Submit Documents**
Documents must be submitted within 15 days of intimation (30 days for health claims).

**Health Insurance Claims:**
- Claim form (available on portal or at branch)
- Hospital discharge summary
- All original hospital bills and receipts
- Investigation and diagnostic reports
- Pharmacy bills with prescriptions
- Doctor's consultation notes
- Pre-authorization letter (for cashless claims)
- ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN card)
- Policy copy

**Motor Insurance Claims:**
- Claim form
- FIR/Police complaint (for theft, accident involving third parties, or total loss)
- Driving license (valid at time of incident)
- Vehicle registration certificate (RC)
- Photographs of the damaged vehicle (minimum 6-8 photos from different angles)
- Repair estimate from authorized service center
- Fitness certificate (for commercial vehicles)

**Home Insurance Claims:**
- Claim form
- FIR/Police report (for theft or burglary)
- Photographs of damage
- List of damaged/lost items with estimated value
- Purchase receipts or bills (for content claims)
- Repair or replacement estimates
- Municipality or fire brigade report (if applicable)

**Life Insurance Claims:**
- Claim form filled by nominee
- Original death certificate
- Original policy document
- Claimant's ID and address proof
- Claimant's bank account details (cancelled cheque)
- Medical records and hospital records (if death due to illness)
- FIR and post-mortem report (if death due to accident or unnatural causes)
- Employer certificate (if applicable)

### 6.3 Claim Status Tracking

You can track your claim status at any time through:
1. **Customer Portal:** Log in at portal.securelife.in → My Claims
2. **Mobile App:** SecureLife app → Claims → Track Status
3. **SMS:** Send CLAIM <CLAIM_NUMBER> to 56789
4. **WhatsApp:** Message +91-98765-43210 with your claim number
5. **Helpline:** Call 1800-123-4567

**Claim Statuses Explained:**
- **Submitted:** Your claim has been registered and assigned a claim number
- **Under Review:** Our team is reviewing your documents and details
- **Surveyor Assigned:** (Motor/Home claims) A surveyor has been assigned to inspect the damage
- **Additional Documents Required:** We need more documents from you — check email/SMS for details
- **Approved:** Your claim has been approved for settlement
- **Denied:** Your claim has been rejected — denial reason will be communicated
- **Payment Initiated:** Payment is being processed
- **Paid/Settled:** Payment has been credited to your bank account
- **Closed:** Claim is fully settled and closed

### 6.4 Claim Denial and Appeals

If your claim is denied, you have the following options:

**Step 1: Understand the Denial**
Review the denial letter carefully. Common reasons include:
- Pre-existing condition not disclosed
- Policy was lapsed or not in force at the time of incident
- Waiting period not completed
- Exclusion clause applicable
- Insufficient or fraudulent documentation

**Step 2: File an Appeal**
- Submit a written appeal within 30 days of denial
- Include additional supporting documents
- Appeals are reviewed by a senior claims officer within 15 days

**Step 3: Escalate to Grievance Cell**
- If appeal is rejected, file a grievance with our Grievance Redressal Officer
- Email: grievance@securelife.in
- Response within 15 working days as per IRDAI guidelines

**Step 4: IRDAI Complaint**
- File a complaint on the IRDAI Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS): igms.irda.gov.in
- Or call IRDAI toll-free: 155255

**Step 5: Insurance Ombudsman**
- Approach the Insurance Ombudsman in your jurisdiction
- For claims up to ₹30 Lakh
- The Ombudsman's decision is binding on the insurer

## 7. Policy Management

### 7.1 Policy Renewal

**Online Renewal:**
1. Log in to portal.securelife.in
2. Go to My Policies → Select the policy to renew
3. Review updated premium (may change based on age, claims, NCB)
4. Make payment via UPI, card, net banking, or NEFT
5. Renewed policy document sent via email within 24 hours

**Grace Period:** 30 days from policy expiry date
- Health insurance: Coverage continues during grace period but renewed policy may have fresh waiting periods if renewed after grace period
- Motor insurance: NCB retained if renewed within 90 days of expiry
- Life insurance: 30 days grace period; policy lapses after that

### 7.2 Policy Portability (Health Insurance)

You can port your health insurance from another insurer to SecureLife without losing waiting period credits.

**How to Port:**
1. Apply at least 45 days before your current policy renewal date
2. Submit portability form with current policy details
3. We will request data from your current insurer via IRDAI's portability platform
4. Waiting period credits will be transferred based on continuous coverage
5. No fresh medical tests required (in most cases)

**What Gets Transferred:**
- Waiting period credits for pre-existing diseases
- Time-bound exclusion credits
- No claim bonus (subject to underwriting)

### 7.3 Updating Policy Details

**What Can Be Updated:**
- Communication address and phone number
- Email address
- Nominee details (with endorsement form)
- Bank account for claim settlements

**What Cannot Be Changed:**
- Policyholder name (requires new policy)
- Date of birth (correction requires documentary proof)
- Sum insured (change takes effect from next renewal)
- Policy start date

### 7.4 Policy Cancellation

**Free-Look Period:** 15 days from receiving policy document
- Full refund of premium minus stamp duty and medical examination charges
- Applicable to all policy types

**After Free-Look Period:**
- Pro-rata refund based on unused coverage period
- Cancellation charges may apply (typically 10-15% of annual premium)
- Health insurance: Consider porting instead of cancelling to preserve waiting period credits

## 9. Tax Benefits

### 9.1 Section 80C — Life Insurance

- Premium paid on life insurance policies qualifies for deduction
- Maximum deduction: ₹1,50,000 per financial year
- Applies to premiums paid for self, spouse, and children
- Premium should not exceed 10% of sum assured (for policies issued after April 2012)

### 9.2 Section 80D — Health Insurance

| Category | Maximum Deduction |
|----------|------------------|
| Self, spouse, children (below 60) | ₹25,000 |
| Self, spouse, children (above 60) | ₹50,000 |
| Parents (below 60) | ₹25,000 |
| Parents (above 60) | ₹50,000 |
| Preventive health checkup | ₹5,000 (within above limits) |

**Maximum combined deduction:** ₹1,00,000 (if all insured persons are senior citizens)

### 9.3 Section 10(10D) — Tax-Free Benefits

- Death benefit under life insurance is completely tax-free
- Maturity proceeds are tax-free if premium does not exceed 10% of sum assured
- Health insurance claim payouts are not taxable
